BREAKING Middle East CRITICAL · 97
US and Iran trade strikes as Hormuz ceasefire frays
The sequence of events is more extended than originally stated and now includes confirmed Iranian strikes on Bahrain and Kuwait. On June 25, Iran struck the container ship Ever Lovely near Oman, prompting US retaliatory strikes on June 26. On June 27, the IRGC struck the Panama-flagged crude tanker Kiku -- carrying over 2 million barrels of QatarEnergy crude and travelling near Ras Al Khaimah off the Omani coastal corridor -- with a one-way attack drone, causing damage to the starboard bridge wing; all crew survived and the vessel continued underway. CENTCOM responded on June 27 with strikes on 10 Iranian military targets including surveillance infrastructure, communications systems, air defence sites, drone storage facilities, and minelayer capabilities. Early on June 28, the IRGC confirmed it launched ballistic missiles and drones at US bases in Bahrain and Kuwait in retaliation, per AP, CBC, and Al Jazeera; Kuwait said air defences intercepted two ballistic missiles with no damage, while Bahrain confirmed an Iranian drone struck a residential building in Muharraq with no fatalities. Iran separately threatened a complete halt to MOU negotiations if US strikes continue. The governing instrument under strain is the June 17 memorandum of understanding signed by Trump and Iranian President Pezeshkian, not an informal ceasefire framework. Trump warned the US could be forced to return to war; the JMIC elevated the Hormuz threat level to substantial as of June 28 (source: AP, CBC, Al Jazeera, PBS, June 28, 2026).

Active US-Iran kinetic exchange with strikes on Gulf basing infrastructure represents the sharpest escalation test of the 2026 ceasefire framework and directly elevates demand signals for THAAD, Patriot, and counter-drone systems in which LMT, RTX, and NOC hold dominant positions.
