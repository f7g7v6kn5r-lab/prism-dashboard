BREAKING North America CRITICAL · 88
FAA Clears Starship Flight 13 After Mishap Probe
The US Federal Aviation Administration on July 13 closed its investigation into the SpaceX Starship Flight 12 Super Heavy booster mishap from the May 22 test flight, issuing clearance for Flight 13 to proceed once remaining safety and licensing requirements are met. SpaceX has announced a target launch date of July 16, 2026, with a 90-minute window opening at 6:45 p.m. EDT from Starbase, Texas. The FAA identified heat effects on propulsion system components and erroneous engine alarm settings as root causes; SpaceX implemented four corrective actions including hardware and software updates. The mission will also carry the first functioning third-generation Starlink V3 satellites to space.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
FAA clearance removes the single largest regulatory gating factor for Starship cadence acceleration, directly affecting timelines for NASA Artemis lunar lander deliveries, Amazon LEO heavy-lift planning, and military heavy-payload concepts dependent on Starship-class vehicles.

▲ BULL CASE
A nominal Flight 13 with successful booster catch and upper stage reentry validates full reusability at scale, reinforcing SpaceX post-listing market cap momentum and pressuring rivals including Rocket Lab and emerging Stoke and Relativity pad developments at the Cape to accelerate timelines to remain competitive for large-payload contracts.

The US Navys first confirmed combat deployment of Saronic Corsair autonomous surface vessels against Irans Bandar Abbas naval facility on July 12 opens a decision window this week on whether CENTCOM expands unmanned surface vehicle strike authority as the maritime blockade of Iranian ports resumes.
