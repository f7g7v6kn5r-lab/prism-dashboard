Starship Flight 13 aborts at ignition below IPO price
SpaceX scrubbed Starship Flight 13 on Jul 16 when the automated flight computer triggered a hold at T-0 during Raptor ignition on Booster 20. SpaceX stated two Raptor engines will be removed and replaced before the next attempt, with the most probable launch timing rescheduled no earlier than Jul 20, 2026 at 10:45 PM UTC (Space.com, Jul 17). The mission will deploy 20 functioning Starlink V3 satellites on a suborbital profile from Orbital Launch Pad 2 at Starbase, Texas. No source confirms SpaceX shares breached a specific IPO price level as a direct result of this scrub; that claim is unverified and omitted.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A failed ignition sequence on the most capital-intensive rocket program in history, occurring within weeks of the largest IPO in financial history, resets expectations for Starlink V3 deployment timelines and introduces execution risk into a stock that the market had priced for near-flawless cadence.

A clean ignition and successful Starship flight restores market confidence in SpaceX operational cadence, stabilises the share price above the 135 USD IPO price, and sustains the record-high space startup funding environment that the SpaceX IPO catalysed for defence-adjacent launch and satellite operators.
▼ BEAR CASE
