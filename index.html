BREAKING North America CRITICAL · 90
Starship Flight 13 aborts on pad at ignition
SpaceX Starship Flight 13, carrying Booster 20 and upper stage Ship 40, was scrubbed on July 17 after an automated abort triggered at T-0 as Raptor engines began igniting. SpaceX stated two Raptor engines will be removed and replaced before the next attempt, with the next launch window now targeted no earlier than July 20, 2026 at 10:45 PM UTC from Orbital Launch Pad 2 at Starbase, Texas. The mission is the second flight of the Starship V3 configuration and would be the first to deploy 20 Starlink V3 satellites on a suborbital trajectory. The abort and subsequent engine replacement have delayed the programme by at least three days; SpaceX has not issued updated valuation guidance in connection with the scrub.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A second consecutive Starship setback delays the Starlink v3 constellation buildout and raises questions about programme cadence reliability at precisely the moment SpaceX is under post-IPO investor scrutiny, amplifying valuation risk for shareholders who priced in an aggressive launch schedule.

BREAKING Middle East CRITICAL · 95
Iran strikes Kuwait and Jordan in seventh war night
Iranian forces launched waves of missiles and drones targeting Kuwait and Jordan during the night of July 17-18, the seventh consecutive night of mutual US-Iran strikes. Kuwaiti air defence systems actively engaged the attacks; a power generation and water desalination plant was hit -- the second such Kuwaiti facility struck in two days -- and Kuwait International Airport suspended flight operations due to repeated threats. Jordan intercepted 10 Iranian missiles with no casualties reported. CENTCOM confirmed two US service members were killed and one is missing in action in Jordan on July 17. Qatar also reported intercepting missiles aimed at Al Udeid Air Base, wounding four people including a child, per WION (July 18). Iran separately launched attacks on Saudi Arabia for the first time in roughly three months, triggering early warnings at Prince Sultan Air Base, according to Reuters citing two people familiar with the matter; Saudi state media did not confirm details. Qatar condemned Iranian attacks on Jordan, Bahrain and Kuwait in the strongest terms; the GCC Secretary-General condemnation cited in the original brief has not been independently confirmed in live sources as of July 18 -- Qatar and Kuwait foreign ministries issued the primary public condemnations on record. Iran declared on July 18 that it is suspending its MOU commitments, accusing the US of violations.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
Iranian strikes reaching Kuwait and Jordan mark a geographic escalation that directly threatens Gulf state stability, US basing arrangements in the region, and the insurance and operational viability of all commercial shipping transiting the Hormuz-Red Sea corridor, with cascading implications for energy markets and regional defence procurement.
