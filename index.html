BREAKING North America CRITICAL · 90
Starship Flight 13 aborts at ignition
SpaceX scrubbed Starship Flight 13 on Jul 16 after an automated abort at T-0 caused by a failure of several Raptor 3 engines to ignite on Booster 20 at Starbase, Texas. The mission is intended to carry 20 Starlink V3 satellites on a suborbital test profile -- not a Starlink constellation-deployment mission in the operational sense, as the satellites will reenter the atmosphere after a brief demonstration. Elon Musk stated on Jul 17 that two Raptor engines will be removed and replaced, with the most probable next launch timing early next week. SPCX stock fell more than 3 percent in after-hours trading on Jul 16, also closing below its IPO price on the session.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A repeated ignition-phase abort on the vehicle SpaceX is counting on for next-generation Starlink v3 deployment raises near-term programme risk for the SDA and commercial broadband customers who depend on Starship cadence to close constellation business cases.

US strikes Iran for sixth consecutive night
US CENTCOM completed a sixth consecutive night of strikes against Iranian targets, hitting six road bridges in Hormozgan province near the Strait of Hormuz and reporting strikes near Bushehr, per Iranian state media and wire sources as of Jul 17. Xi launches global AI alliance at summit
Xi Jinping spoke at the opening of the World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC) in Shanghai on Jul 17, his first appearance at the event, calling for AI development to be a global cooperative effort rather than dominated by any single country. The World Artificial Intelligence Cooperation Organisation (WAICO) -- a 29-nation intergovernmental body headquartered in Shanghai -- was formally constituted on Jul 16, a day before Xi spoke; the alliance was announced by Beijing at the conference, not personally launched by Xi in his keynote. Member founding countries include Indonesia, Brazil, Malaysia, South Africa, Russia and Pakistan, with analysts noting Beijing is likely to use WAICO to shape AI regulation at the UN.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A China-led AI alliance competing with US and European AI governance frameworks will fragment global AI standards, forcing defence and space operators to make explicit technology alignment choices with long-term supply chain consequences.
