BREAKING North America CRITICAL · 85
SpaceX Starfall Reentry Demo Launches Today
SpaceX is targeting 06:43 EDT (10:43 UTC) on 23 June 2026 for the Starfall Demo mission, launched on a Falcon 9 from Space Launch Complex 40 at Cape Canaveral Space Force Station in an uncrewed configuration; a backup window is available on 24 June. Starfall is a disk-shaped cargo-return capsule 3.1 m wide and 0.75 m tall, capable of returning up to 1,000 kg of payload from orbit, and is not a crewed or Dragon-derived vehicle. The FAA granted environmental approval in May 2026 for up to two demonstration reentries into the Pacific Ocean approximately 1,300 km off the US West Coast. NASASpaceFlight and SpaceFlightNow are providing live coverage. This is the first public test of the Starfall capsule, targeting orbital manufacturing and point-to-point cargo delivery markets. As of the time of this brief the launch had not yet occurred; no scrub or delay had been reported. (Sources: NASASpaceFlight, Space.com, SpaceFlightNow, 22-23 June 2026.)
◈ A successful Starfall demonstration would establish SpaceX as a competitor in the dedicated reentry and capsule market, with direct implications for government and commercial customers currently reliant on Dragon or foreign providers.
