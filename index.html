Starship Flight 13 aborts on pad at ignition
SpaceX Starship Flight 13 suffered an automated abort at T-0 on Jul 16, 2026, when the onboard flight computer triggered a hold as Raptor engines began to ignite, according to Space.com. SpaceX stated two Raptor engines would be removed and replaced. The next launch attempt is targeted no earlier than Jul 20 at 10:45 PM UTC from Orbital Launch Pad 2 at Starbase, Texas. The flight, the second of the Starship V3 configuration, is intended to deploy 20 Starlink V3 satellites on a suborbital trajectory, making the scrub a delay — not a failure — for both the V3 constellation rollout and Starship maturation.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
Repeated Starship development delays widen the gap between the Space Forces expanded 17 billion dollar launch contract ambitions and the actual heavy-lift capacity available to fulfil them, sustaining near-term pricing power for Falcon 9 and Electron.
