US Senate votes to halt Iran war operations
The US Senate voted 50-48 on Jun 23 to pass a concurrent war powers resolution directing the president to remove US forces from hostilities against Iran, joining a House vote of 215-208 earlier in June. However, because it was introduced as a concurrent resolution, it does not go to the president for signature and carries no force of law; the White House dismissed it on Jun 23 as having no legal significance. ◈ A sustained war powers standoff between Congress and the executive branch introduces material uncertainty into the forward defence procurement pipeline, particularly for munitions, directed energy, and space-based ISR programmes that were accelerated under active-conflict authorisations.
