BREAKING Middle East CRITICAL · 95
US enforces Iran maritime blockade from Tuesday
This is a reinstated blockade, not the original. The US Navy lifted a prior blockade on June 18 after Trump and Iranian president Pezeshkian signed a memorandum of understanding on June 17. Trump announced reinstatement on July 13 and CENTCOM confirmed enforcement began July 14 at 2000 GMT, covering all Iranian ports, oil terminals and coastal areas for all vessel traffic regardless of flag (Reuters, July 13). Neutral transit through the Strait of Hormuz to non-Iranian destinations is not impeded. Trump separately announced a 20% toll on cargo shipped through the strait; CENTCOM did not reference fees in its enforcement advisory. Iran has struck Gulf tankers in response and the Houthis have threatened fresh attacks, as of this news pull.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A formal maritime blockade combined with active tanker strikes creates the highest near-term threat level to Gulf energy and commercial shipping since 2019, with direct implications for satellite-based maritime surveillance demand, naval drone procurement, and energy-linked defence budgets across the GCC.
