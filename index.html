BREAKING North America CRITICAL · 90
Starship Flight 13 second attempt underway
Starship Flight 13 second launch attempt is scheduled for today, Jul 23, with a 90-minute window opening at 5:45 p.m. CT (10:45 PM UTC), per SpaceX and Space.com (Jul 23). The Jul 16 abort was caused by four Raptor engines failing to meet startup parameters -- not a post-ignition anomaly -- and the fix required replacement of two Raptor 3 engines, not an additional Booster 20 static fire test. The mission is configured to deploy 20 first-generation Starlink V3 satellites on a suborbital flight.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A successful Flight 13 would unlock Starlink v3 capacity at scale, directly strengthening the orbital communications backbone that NATO HALO and allied military constellations depend on for interoperability — a failed second attempt would extend the gap in US heavy-lift credibility at a moment of peak strategic demand.

URGENT North America CRITICAL · 85
Northrop MRV-1 robot launched to service GEO satellites
SpaceX launched Northrop Grumman MRV-1, a two-armed robotic servicing spacecraft, from Cape Canaveral on Tuesday evening on a DARPA-backed decade-long mission to install propulsion pods on three aging commercial satellites in geostationary orbit. The vehicle features swiveling dual arms designed to mechanically mate with and extend the operational life of out-of-fuel GEO assets, representing the first commercial deployment of this servicing architecture.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
MRV-1 operationalises on-orbit satellite servicing as a commercial and defence-relevant capability, directly reducing the cost and timeline of replacing aging GEO intelligence and communications satellites and signalling a structural shift in how operators manage constellation lifecycle risk.
