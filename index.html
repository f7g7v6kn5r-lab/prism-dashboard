Hormuz ceasefire collapses amid fresh US-Iran strikes
The Islamabad Memorandum of Understanding, signed by the US and Iranian presidents on June 17 2026, has not been formally cancelled but has been severely undermined. President Trump declared the ceasefire over on July 7 2026 following Iranian attacks on three commercial vessels, though he subsequently said the exchange of fire would not lead to long-term military action. The conflict should be characterised as an MOU under severe stress with active kinetic exchanges, not a fully collapsed ceasefire agreement.
