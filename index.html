IRGC Warns Ships Against New Hormuz Route
The IRGC Navy on June 25, 2026 warned commercial vessels to use only Iran-approved transit routes through the Strait of Hormuz, rejecting a separate corridor announced by Oman in coordination with the IMO without prior notification to Tehran. The IRGC stated that vessels using unapproved routes will face enforcement action and that mandatory Channel 16 coordination with the IRGC Navy is required for all transits. The dispute is identified as an unresolved issue following a memorandum of understanding signed by the United States and Iran, which largely halted hostilities; it does not constitute a new routing regime imposed by Iran but rather a rejection of a third-party route announced without Iranian coordination.
