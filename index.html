Starship Flight 13 Launches With Starlink V3 Payload
As of July 16, SpaceX has not yet launched Starship Flight 13; the 90-minute window opens at 6:45 p.m. EDT (2245 GMT) from Starbase, Texas, with the vehicle stacked and status listed as GO per SpaceX. The FAA closed its Flight 12 mishap investigation on July 13, clearing launch preparations to proceed. The critical engineering objective is an in-space relight of a single Raptor Vacuum engine -- not all Raptor Vacuum engines -- which NASA requires as a gate before crewed Artemis lunar missions can proceed. The mission will also attempt to deploy 20 functioning Starlink V3 satellites on a suborbital trajectory; the satellites are expected to reenter and demise after a brief demonstration.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A successful Raptor relight removes the single largest technical barrier between Starship and crewed lunar operations, directly affecting the valuation trajectory of SpaceX and the timeline of NASAs Human Landing System programme.
