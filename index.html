US-Iran ceasefire collapses amid intensifying strikes
The Islamabad MOU signed in mid-June 2026 between the United States and Iran has effectively collapsed. President Trump declared the ceasefire over on Jul 8, 2026, speaking at the NATO summit in Ankara, Turkey, after Iran attacked three commercial vessels in the Strait of Hormuz (Washington Times, Jul 8, 2026). The US military struck approximately 90 Iranian targets along the coastline over two consecutive days; Iran retaliated with strikes on US military bases in Kuwait, Bahrain, and Jordan, the last of which Jordan said it intercepted (NBC News, CNN, Jul 9, 2026). Trump said he did not want a full return to war and suggested negotiations could still continue, but direct talks appear increasingly unlikely (NBC News, Jul 9, 2026). Strait of Hormuz traffic came to a near standstill, with just 13 commercial vessels transiting in the 24 hours to Jul 9 versus a pre-war baseline of roughly 110 per day (MarineTraffic data via CNN, Jul 9, 2026). The UK government warning cited in the original brief has not been independently confirmed by live sources; the confirmed UK position as of Jul 8 is that Prime Minister Starmer urged both parties at the NATO summit to respect the ceasefire (Washington Times, Jul 8, 2026).
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A return to sustained US-Iran warfare creates immediate demand for theatre missile defence, counter-drone systems, maritime domain awareness, and GPS-resilient navigation across all Gulf-based allied force postures and commercial shipping operators.
