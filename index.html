China fires SLBM in South Pacific in nuclear signal
China test-launched a submarine-launched ballistic missile carrying a dummy warhead from a nuclear-powered submarine on July 6 2026, with the missile transiting the Pacific and impacting in designated waters near the South Pacific Nuclear Free Zone -- this is Chinas first publicly acknowledged SLBM launch into international open waters. The missile type is unconfirmed by Beijing but assessed by analysts as likely a JL-2 or JL-3; the launch originated from the South China Sea or Bohai Sea area, not from the South Pacific itself. The test coincided with the start of the China-Russia Joint Sea-2026 naval exercise and was not timed alongside US special forces deployments in Taiwan -- no source connects it to that trigger.
