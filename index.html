BREAKING North America CRITICAL · 90
Starship Flight 13 Aborts at Ignition
SpaceX scrubbed its 13th Starship and Super Heavy test flight on the evening of Jul 16 after an automated abort triggered at T-0, with the flight computer halting ignition of the Super Heavy booster Raptors. The abort was the first Starship launch attempt since SpaceX went public on Jun 12. Elon Musk posted on Jul 17 that a failure of a few Raptor 3 engines to light caused the abort, that two engines will be removed and replaced, and that the most probable next launch timing is early next week; no specific date has been confirmed as of Jul 17. The mission is intended to carry 20 functioning Starlink V3 satellites on a suborbital trajectory -- not the first Starlink v3 satellites, as prior flights carried mass simulators of the same design.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A post-IPO launch abort places SpaceX operational credibility under immediate investor scrutiny and creates near-term opportunity for rival launch providers competing for national security and commercial payloads.

US Strikes Iran for Sixth Night Running
US Central Command confirmed a sixth consecutive night of strikes against Iranian military and infrastructure targets on the night of Jul 16-17, hitting multiple road bridges in Hormozgan province, a railway junction in Bandar Abbas, and power infrastructure, according to Iranian state media and CNN (updated Jul 17). Iranian state media reported at least seven killed and 20 wounded in the latest strikes; Iranian health officials put the cumulative toll since the US restarted strikes in early July at 38 killed and over 400 wounded. Iran has responded by launching strikes on five Persian Gulf states including Qatar, Kuwait, Jordan, Bahrain, and Syria. No ceasefire agreement is in effect; the MOU signed Jun 17 was declared over by Trump on Jul 8 and a subsequent interim ceasefire collapsed on Jul 8 as well.
