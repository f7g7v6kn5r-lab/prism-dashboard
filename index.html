Hormuz and Bab al-Mandeb close; Gulf shipping disrupted
As of July 21-22, 2026, the Strait of Hormuz is effectively closed to commercial shipping, with transits at roughly 10 vessels per day against a pre-war baseline of approximately 88 per day. The Bab al-Mandeb is not confirmed as fully closed; on July 20, 2026, the Houthis announced a maritime blockade on Saudi shipping through the strait and issued warnings on open maritime frequencies, but a full physical closure has not been independently confirmed. The two situations are distinct: Hormuz is effectively closed by the US-Iran conflict; Bab al-Mandeb faces a declared Houthi embargo on Saudi shipping that threatens but has not yet achieved full closure (sources: straits.live Jul 21, 2026; The War Zone Jul 21, 2026; Fortune Jul 21, 2026).
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
Simultaneous closure of the two most critical maritime chokepoints for Gulf energy exports creates acute supply chain and energy price risk that directly affects defence logistics, fuel cost assumptions, and the economic resilience of US regional allies hosting American forces.
