Rocket Lab to acquire Iridium in landmark deal
Rocket Lab and Iridium Communications announced on June 29 2026 that they have entered into a definitive agreement under which Rocket Lab will acquire Iridium in a cash-and-stock transaction valued at approximately 8.0 billion dollars, or 54 dollars per Iridium share -- a 24.1 percent premium to Iridiums last close. The deal combines Rocket Labs launch and spacecraft manufacturing capabilities with Iridiums 66-satellite LEO constellation, globally licensed L-band spectrum, and a subscriber base of more than 2.55 million across government, defence, aviation, maritime, and commercial markets. Both boards have unanimously approved the agreement; completion is subject to Iridium shareholder and regulatory approvals and is expected in mid-2027. The agreement constitutes a definitive merger agreement, not a completed acquisition.

US and Iran Halt Strikes, Resume Doha Talks
As of June 28 2026, the United States and Iran agreed to halt tit-for-tat strikes and schedule technical talks in Doha, Qatar, for Tuesday June 30 (not resuming previously suspended Doha talks -- this is a newly scheduled session). The standdown followed five days of mutual strikes, including Iranian missile attacks on US military facilities in Kuwait and Bahrain and US strikes on Iranian military surveillance, air defence, drone storage, and minelayer infrastructure. The underlying framework is the Islamabad Memorandum of Understanding signed June 17 2026 by both presidents; neither side has formally declared the MOU void as of June 29. The MOU established a 60-day ceasefire period, but the Hormuz transit corridor dispute -- specifically which shipping lane is legally authorised -- remains unresolved and was the proximate trigger for the five days of strikes. Iran continues to assert control over Hormuz passage; the June 30 Doha session will address all areas of the MOU, per a US official cited by RFE/RL on June 28 2026.
