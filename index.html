BREAKING North America CRITICAL · 85
Starship Flight 13 Aborts, V3 Satellites On Hold
Starship Flight 13 aborted on 16 July after four engines on Booster 20 failed to ignite during the post-ignition sequence, forcing SpaceX to stand down. SpaceX replaced two Raptor engines and retargeted a second launch attempt for no earlier than 23 July 2026, with a 90-minute window opening at 5:45 p.m. CT, per SpaceX and Space.com as of 21 July. The abort is attributed to a post-ignition engine anomaly, not a booster failure as such. Falcon 9 continues to sustain the existing Starlink constellation in the interim, but V3 capacity -- including gigabit-class throughput -- remains unavailable until Starship successfully completes a payload mission; the 20 V3 satellites aboard Flight 13 are on a suborbital trajectory and will demise on reentry approximately 20 minutes after deployment.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
Starship delays directly postpone Starlink V3 network upgrades that underpin both commercial broadband ambitions and military communications contracts, creating execution risk across SpaceX revenue streams and downstream ASTS and RKLB investor sentiment.

US Soldiers Killed in Iranian Attack on Jordan Base
Two US Army soldiers -- Private Isabella Gonzales, 19, of Carrollton, Texas, and 1st Lt. Tyler James Feehan, 25, of Ewa Beach, Hawaii -- were killed at Muwaffaq Salti Air Base in Jordan during an Iranian ballistic missile and drone barrage on 18 July, confirmed by the Pentagon on 20 July.
