Starship F13 flies today with Raptor relight gate
SpaceX is targeting a launch window opening at 6:45 p.m. EDT on Jul 16 for Starship Flight 13 from Starbase, Texas (Space.com, Jul 16). Multiple credible sources confirm the primary engineering objectives are deployment of the first 20 Starlink V3 satellites and heat shield validation testing -- not a Raptor Vacuum engine relight, which no current source identifies as a stated gate for this flight. Six of the V3 satellites carry cameras to relay heat shield imagery to engineers on the ground. The Super Heavy booster is planned for a controlled splashdown in the Gulf of Mexico rather than a launch tower catch. SpaceX has described Flight 13 as targeting similar objectives to Flight 12, which debuted the V3 vehicle configuration on May 22, 2026.
