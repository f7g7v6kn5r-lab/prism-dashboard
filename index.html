Hormuz Reopens as US-Iran Deal Takes Effect
President Trump and Iranian President Pezeshkian signed a memorandum of understanding at Versailles on Jun 17, committing to end fighting and reopen the Strait of Hormuz. Partial transit resumed via northern and southern routes, but the main central channel remained closed as of Jun 18 due to an estimated 80 mines requiring clearance. However, as of Jun 20 Iran declared the Strait of Hormuz closed again, citing Israeli ceasefire violations in Lebanon (NBC News, Jun 20). Follow-on technical talks at Burgenstock, Switzerland were postponed on Jun 19 after the Swiss Foreign Ministry confirmed they would not proceed; mediators are working to reschedule. US envoy Steve Witkoff is part of the US negotiating team; VP Vance had been expected to travel to Switzerland but postponed that trip.
◈ Reopening of Hormuz stabilises global energy supply chains critical to defence logistics and reduces near-term risk premiums priced into Gulf-exposed aerospace and maritime security contracts.
