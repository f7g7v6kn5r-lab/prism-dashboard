BREAKING North America CRITICAL · 97
Rocket Lab to acquire Iridium in LEO power move
Rocket Lab and Iridium Communications announced on June 29, 2026, that they have entered into a definitive agreement under which Rocket Lab will acquire Iridium at $54 per share ($27 cash plus the remainder in Rocket Lab stock), representing an enterprise value of approximately $8 billion. The deal, filed with the SEC via Form 8-K and Form 425, unites Rocket Labs launch and satellite manufacturing capabilities with Iridiums 66-satellite LEO network, spectrum, and more than 2.55 million active subscribers. The transaction is expected to close by mid-2027, subject to Iridium shareholder approval and customary regulatory clearances; a proxy statement and Form S-4 registration are forthcoming. CEO Peter Beck has described the combined entity as positioned to scale into untapped markets and pioneer new space-based services.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
This is the most significant consolidation event in commercial LEO since Starlink internalized manufacturing and launch, and it forces every sovereign operator, constellation investor, and government customer to reassess whether fragmented single-layer strategies remain viable against full-stack competitors.
