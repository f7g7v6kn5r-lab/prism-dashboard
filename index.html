China fires SLBM into South Pacific in historic first
On July 6, 2026, a Chinese Navy nuclear-powered submarine launched a ballistic missile -- carrying a dummy warhead -- from the South China Sea on a trajectory that overflew the Philippines before splashing down in the Pacific Ocean approximately 300 km east of Tonga, according to USNI News and CSIS (July 6-9, 2026). China described the test as routine annual training and stated that selected regional governments, including Australia, Japan, New Zealand and Papua New Guinea, were notified in advance, though several said notification came only hours beforehand. The test coincided with the start of the annual China-Russia Joint Sea 2026 naval exercise in Qingdao. The missile type has not been officially confirmed; independent analysts suggest it was either a JL-2 or JL-3. The US, Japan, Australia, and New Zealand criticised the test. The test occurred on July 6, not during the current US-Iran escalation cycle, and no source directly links it to that conflict.
