US-Iran ceasefire collapses, fresh strikes imminent
President Trump declared the US-Iran memorandum of understanding effectively over on July 8 after Iranian forces attacked three commercial vessels in the Strait of Hormuz between July 6-7. Iran retaliated by targeting Gulf Arab states including Bahrain, Kuwait, and Qatar. Trump stated at the NATO summit in Ankara on July 8 that the MOU was over but that talks could continue, adding he did not know if Iran was worthy of a deal; he did not refuse to depart the summit and flew home on an older Air Force One, stopping at RAF Mildenhall before boarding the new Qatari-gifted plane for the return to the US. The Islamabad MOU signed June 17 has not been formally rescinded and negotiations have not been officially terminated as of July 9.
