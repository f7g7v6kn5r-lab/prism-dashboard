Starship Flight 13 launches with Raptor relight test
SpaceX scheduled the 13th test flight of Starship and Super Heavy from Starbase, Texas for July 16, 2026, with a 90-minute launch window opening at 6:45 p.m. EDT (2245 GMT), per SpaceX and Space.com as of July 16. The mission will deploy 20 functioning Starlink V3 satellites -- not mass simulators intended to burn up -- which will extend solar arrays and antennas and attempt to connect with ground stations in South Africa and the broader Starlink constellation via laser links before reentering on the same suborbital trajectory as the vehicle. A relight of a single Raptor engine while in space remains a listed upper-stage objective alongside a controlled splashdown in the Indian Ocean, per NextSpaceflight as of July 16. The FAA closed its Flight 12 mishap investigation and accepted SpaceX findings on July 13, clearing launch preparations to proceed, per SpaceNews.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
Successful Raptor relight would unlock the critical path to crewed Artemis lunar missions and materially de-risk the NASA HLS contract timeline, with direct consequences for both SpaceX programme revenue and competing lunar architecture investments.
