Diplomatic fragility and defence spending acceleration are converging into a single structural signal: the US-Iran Switzerland talks, now into their first round of implementation negotiations at Burgenstock after a Friday postponement and Sunday resumption (as of Jun 22, Swiss FDFA), are advancing on the 60-day MOU roadmap signed electronically by Trump and Iranian President Pezeshkian on Jun 19, though the process remains strained by continued Israeli attacks in Lebanon. The contradiction is stark: defence equities are selling off as if peace is coming, yet the Pentagon is budgeting for sustained conflict, a divergence that signals either a mis-priced defence sector or a market that correctly reads the MOU as structurally durable. Watch whether the first round of Burgenstock implementation talks, which began Jun 21, produces concrete steps on the MOU -- progress would validate the market de-escalation thesis, while a breakdown over Irans demand that Israeli ceasefire violations be addressed first would create acute upside risk for defence primes from deeply discounted last-close levels.
