BREAKING Asia-Pacific CRITICAL · 95
China fires ballistic missile into South Pacific
Chinas navy test-launched a long-range ballistic missile from a nuclear-powered submarine into the South Pacific on July 7, carrying a dummy warhead -- the first publicly acknowledged submarine-launched test to travel this far into the Pacific, according to analyst Lyle Morris of the Asia Society Policy Institute (NPR, July 7). It is not the first Chinese missile test in the Pacific in decades: China last conducted a Pacific missile test in 2024, firing a land-based ICBM with a dummy warhead, the first such land-based test since 1980. Japan, New Zealand, and Australia received advance notifications; the US did not. The launch occurred on the same day Australia and Fiji signed a mutual defence treaty widely interpreted as a direct counter to Chinese influence in the Pacific.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
China's deliberate timing of an ICBM test to coincide with a new Australia-Fiji defence pact signals a calculated deterrence message directed at the emerging US-aligned Pacific security architecture, accelerating the case for allied investment in regional ballistic missile defence, satellite-based early warning, and space domain awareness capabilities.

BREAKING Middle East CRITICAL · 93
Unknown projectile hits tanker near Hormuz strait
A liquefied natural gas tanker was struck by a projectile inside the Strait of Hormuz near Limah, Oman, on July 7, catching fire, according to the UK Maritime Trade Operations centre (UKMTO), which confirmed the projectile hit the port side of the vessel as it traveled south out of the strait toward the Gulf of Oman; no environmental impact was reported and no casualties were confirmed in initial reporting (AP, July 7). Iranian state television implied Tehran carried out the assault, stating the tanker ignored warnings, but no official Iranian claim had been made as of July 7. No US-Iran ceasefire is in effect; US-Iran nuclear talks remain active though unresolved, and Trump reiterated military threats toward Iran on July 7.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
An unattributed maritime strike near Hormuz at this precise moment in US-Iran negotiations injects acute ambiguity into the ceasefire, and any confirmed Iranian or proxy attribution would immediately reprice Gulf shipping risk, energy security insurance premiums, and the defence equities most exposed to a Hormuz escalation scenario.
