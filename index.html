BREAKING North America CRITICAL · 90
SpaceX launches Northrop MRV-1 servicing craft
SpaceX successfully launched Northrop Grumman SpaceLogistics Mission Robotic Vehicle 1 (MRV-1) from Cape Canaveral Space Force Station SLC-40 on July 21, 2026 at 5:15 p.m. ET. The mission carries the MRV and three Mission Extension Pods (MEPs) -- not propulsion pods described as jetpacks -- to geostationary transfer orbit. The MEPs are 400 kg spacecraft designed to be captured by the MRV and attached to client satellites to extend service life by up to six years per pod. Two MEPs will service Intelsat satellites and one will service an Optus satellite. The mission is backed by DARPA and the Naval Research Laboratory; the robotic arms were developed in partnership with NRL and NASA. The booster B1069 was expended and not recovered.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
MRV-1 establishes a commercial precedent for on-orbit servicing that could extend the operational and revenue life of billions of dollars worth of GEO communication assets, directly benefiting satellite operators and opening a new addressable market for Northrop Grumman in space logistics.

Trump approves nuclear deal allowing Saudi enrichment
President Trump has approved a nuclear agreement with Saudi Arabia that, according to two AP sources confirmed July 22, 2026, could potentially provide the kingdom with uranium enrichment capability for its civilian nuclear program. The 30-year deal, expected to be formally announced as early as Wednesday July 22, would involve US firms in developing the program and is expected to be submitted to Congress for review. The deal could allow construction of a uranium enrichment facility in Saudi Arabia following a joint US-Saudi study, but bars Riyadh from developing its own enrichment technology or purchasing it from other countries for a decade. The agreement is not expected to include the IEAs Additional Protocol for enhanced monitoring, raising proliferation concerns among lawmakers and arms control experts.
