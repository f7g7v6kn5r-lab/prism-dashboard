China recovers orbital booster with Long March 10B
China launched its Long March 10B rocket on 10 July 2026 and successfully recovered the first stage booster using a net-based sea platform, making China the second country -- and the first outside the United States -- to recover an orbital-class rocket booster after launch (SpaceNews, 10 July 2026). Wikipedia and multiple sources note that Long March 10B is the sixth launcher system globally to achieve first-stage recovery during an orbital launch attempt, after the Space Shuttle, Falcon 9, Electron, Starship, and Blue Origin New Glenn -- meaning the brief claim that China is the second country in history to do so is broadly accurate, but Blue Origin New Glenn (November 2025) had already made the US the country with two separate organisations achieving this before China. The more precise framing as of 10 July 2026 is that China is the first country outside the United States to recover an orbital booster, and CASC claimed the world-first distinction specifically for net-based recovery. The achievement remains a decisive step in Chinas reusable launch vehicle programme and directly challenges SpaceX on cost-competitive heavy reusable lift.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
China acquiring orbital booster recovery capability removes a core Western commercial and military launch cost advantage and raises the strategic stakes for US and allied launch programmes competing for government and commercial manifest share.
