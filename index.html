US Senate Votes to Halt Iran War
The US Senate passed a war powers concurrent resolution 50-48 on Jun 23, with four Republicans -- Rand Paul, Susan Collins, Lisa Murkowski, and Bill Cassidy -- joining nearly all Democrats, directing Trump to remove US forces from hostilities with Iran unless explicitly authorised by Congress. The measure is the first war powers resolution to clear both chambers, having passed the House 215-208 on Jun 3. However, as a concurrent resolution it does not require a presidential signature and the White House stated on Jun 23 that it has no legal force, meaning no veto is possible or expected. The constitutional dispute over its binding effect remains open, with some Democratic lawmakers indicating they will explore legal avenues to compel compliance.
◈ Congressional action to constrain executive war authority introduces legislative risk into defence procurement timelines, particularly for missile and air defence programmes justified by the Iran conflict, and investors should monitor whether a presidential veto sustains or disrupts the rearmament spending thesis.

Germany Scraps F126 Frigate, Pivots to MEKO
Germany formally cancelled the F126 frigate programme on Jun 24, scrapping plans to build six anti-submarine warships originally contracted to Damen and later transferred to Rheinmetall subsidiary NVL. The Defence Ministry announced it will instead procure eight MEKO A-200 frigates from ThyssenKrupp Marine Systems, with the first four hulls priced at approximately EUR 6.3 billion subject to Bundestag budget committee approval. Continuing the F126 programme would have exceeded EUR 18 billion; the cancelled programme had already incurred approximately EUR 2.3 billion in sunk costs. Rheinmetall shares fell as much as 16 percent on the day, while TKMS shares rose approximately 10 percent. Legal review of potential damage claims against Damen is ongoing.
◈ Germanys abrupt pivot signals that European navies are prioritising cost-proven platforms over ambitious next-generation programmes, creating near-term risk for contractors dependent on large single-nation frigate orders and opportunities for MEKO-capable yards.
