Rocket Lab acquires Iridium for $8 billion
Rocket Lab announced a definitive agreement on 29 June 2026 to acquire Iridium Communications in a cash-and-stock transaction valued at approximately $8 billion ($54 per share, half cash and half Rocket Lab stock), transforming the company from a launch provider into a vertically integrated space-services business with a 66-satellite LEO network serving more than 2.55 million subscribers. The transaction is pending Iridium stockholder and regulatory approvals and is expected to close in mid-2027; it has not yet closed. Both boards unanimously approved the deal. CEO Peter Beck described the move as uniting Rocket Labs launch and satellite manufacturing capabilities with Iridiums global network and spectrum.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
This acquisition redefines Rocket Labs competitive position from a subscale launch provider to a full-spectrum space-services company, directly challenging legacy operators and signaling that vertical integration — not launch cadence alone — will determine who captures the next decade of space revenue.
