Rocket Lab to Acquire Iridium
Rocket Lab and Iridium Communications announced on June 29, 2026 that they have entered into a definitive agreement -- not a completed acquisition -- under which Rocket Lab will acquire all outstanding Iridium shares at $54 per share in a cash-and-stock transaction representing an enterprise value of approximately $8.0 billion. The deal combines Rocket Labs launch capability and satellite manufacturing platform with Iridiums 66-satellite LEO constellation, globally licensed L-band spectrum, and a customer base of more than 2.55 million subscribers across government, defence, aviation, maritime, and commercial markets. Closing is targeted for mid-2027, pending Iridium stockholder and regulatory approvals; both boards have unanimously approved the transaction (SEC 8-K filings, Jun 29, 2026).
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
This acquisition transforms Rocket Lab from a launch and spacecraft services provider into a vertically integrated space communications operator, directly challenging Starlink and Amazon LEO for government and enterprise contracts and setting a consolidation precedent that other new-space firms will face pressure to match.
