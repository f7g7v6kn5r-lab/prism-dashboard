Space Force Triples Launch Contract to $17B
The US Space Force raised the maximum value of its National Security Space Launch Phase 3 Lane 1 contract from 5.6 billion dollars to 17 billion dollars, citing rising demand for assured access to orbit. This tripling of the contract ceiling represents one of the largest single expansions of US government launch procurement authority in the programme's history and signals sustained institutional pull for national security payloads.

BREAKING North America CRITICAL · 88
Starship Flight 13 Aborts at Ignition
SpaceX Starship Flight 13 aborted at T-0 on July 16, 2026, after an automated flight computer abort call when multiple Raptor 3 engines on Booster 20 failed to ignite, according to Space.com and CNN. Elon Musk confirmed two engines needed replacement and indicated the next attempt would be early the following week. As of July 19, the next launch attempt is scheduled no earlier than July 20, 2026 at 10:45 PM UTC from Starbase, Texas. The abort does not affect the mission profile: Flight 13 remains targeted at deploying 20 functioning Starlink V3 satellites on a suborbital trajectory, the second flight of the Starship V3 configuration.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A repeated ignition-phase abort on Starship delays the operational transition of the world's most capable heavy-lift vehicle and introduces uncertainty into the timelines of payloads — including national security and commercial customers — that have been sequenced around Starship's flight cadence.
