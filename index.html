BREAKING Middle East CRITICAL · 95
US Strikes Iran After Hormuz Tanker Attacks
On Jul 7, the IRGC struck three commercial vessels transiting the Strait of Hormuz -- the Qatari LNG tanker Al Rekayyat, a Saudi-flagged supertanker, and a third vessel hit by drone. CENTCOM launched retaliatory strikes targeting Iranian air defences, radar sites, anti-ship missile sites, and IRGC small boats. The US Treasury simultaneously revoked General License X, the sanctions waiver authorising Iranian oil sales under the Islamabad Memorandum of Understanding, replacing it with the narrower General License X1 which bars new Iranian oil sales after Jul 7 and requires wind-down of existing transactions by Jul 17. Trump, speaking at the NATO Ankara summit on Jul 8, declared the MoU over, though Iran has not yet formally confirmed that position; Iranian negotiators characterised the waiver revocation as a violation of Article 10 of the MoU and Iran claimed strikes on 85 US military facilities in Bahrain and Kuwait in retaliation. US officials stated that negotiations toward a final agreement continue. The MoU itself has not been formally terminated by both parties as of Jul 8.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
The resumption of direct US-Iran kinetic exchanges and the oil sales revocation together signal that the Strait of Hormuz remains an active conflict choke point, creating sustained upward pressure on oil prices and a strong near-term demand signal for naval missile defence, maritime patrol, and ISR systems across all primes.
