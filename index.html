Hormuz Reopens But Iran Deal Faces Fresh Risks
The US-Iran MOU formal signing is scheduled for June 19 in Geneva -- not at the Palace of Versailles on June 18 as previously stated. CENTCOM announced on June 18 that it had lifted all restrictions on commercial vessels entering and exiting Iranian ports and coastal areas ahead of the signing, confirming the naval blockade has been lifted. The MOU initiates a 60-day negotiation period covering uranium enrichment limits, sanctions relief, and reconstruction funding; key issues including Irans ballistic missile programme were explicitly deferred. Physical normalization of shipping will take considerably longer due to mine clearance requirements estimated at up to six months and a tanker backlog built over nearly four months. Disputes over unresolved nuclear terms, Israeli-Lebanese withdrawal conditions, and hardening positions on both sides remain risks to the follow-on negotiations. (Sources: TechTimes, NBC News, India.com, Axios, CNN, June 14-19, 2026)
◈ Reopening of Hormuz stabilises near-term energy supply chains critical to Gulf-based defence logistics, but unresolved implementation disputes create persistent risk premiums for regional operators and investors.
