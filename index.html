Starship Flight 13 aborts on engine anomaly
SpaceX aborted Starship Flight 13 at T-0 on Jul 16 after four of Booster 20s 33 Raptor engines failed to ignite, triggering an automatic abort. Elon Musk confirmed on Jul 17 that two Raptor engines will be removed and replaced, with the most probable next launch timing early the week of Jul 21; no firm date has been set as of Jul 18. The root cause has been identified at the engine level; no destacking has been confirmed as necessary. SpaceX stock (SPCX) closed at 131.11 on Jul 16, below its 135 IPO price, and fell a further approximately 3 percent in after-hours trading. The brief does not accurately characterise the anomaly as a single-engine issue: at least four engines failed to ignite. The characterisation of a trillion-dollar drawdown from peak is not supported by available sourcing; shares had erased gains from an earlier post-IPO surge but specific peak-to-trough figures should not be stated without a confirmed source.
