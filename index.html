Starship Flight 13 aborts on pad at ignition
SpaceX Starship Flight 13 suffered a post-ignition anomaly on July 16 2026, with the automated flight computer initiating a hold and abort call at T-0 as Raptor engines were starting to ignite, scrubbing what was planned as the second flight of the Starship V3 configuration intended to deploy 20 Starlink V3 satellites on a suborbital trajectory. SpaceX stated two Raptor engines would be removed and replaced, with the next launch attempt now targeted for no earlier than July 20 2026 at 10:45 PM UTC, per Space.com and RocketLaunch.Live as of July 19 2026. The abort follows the previously completed static fire of Booster 20, which had successfully tested all 33 engines. The brief does not characterize this as part of a broader pattern of setbacks -- it was a single-flight scrub followed promptly by a rescheduled attempt.
