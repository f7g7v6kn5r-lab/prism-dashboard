US Launches Third Night of Iran Strikes; Blockade Reimposed
The United States launched a third consecutive night of precision strikes across Iran on July 14 as President Trump reimposed a naval blockade on Iranian ports effective 4 p.m. ET. Trump separately announced and then reversed a proposed 20 percent toll on cargo transiting the Strait of Hormuz on the same day, replacing it with trade and investment deals from Gulf states; no further details on those deals have been provided (NPR, July 14). On July 6, a Chinese nuclear-powered submarine launched a submarine-launched ballistic missile -- assessed by CSIS and multiple analysts as likely a JL-2 or JL-3 -- carrying a dummy warhead approximately 7,300 kilometers from the South China Sea into designated waters in the South Pacific, in what CSIS describes as the first time China has ever launched an SLBM into international open waters. Beijing described the launch as routine annual training and stated relevant nations were notified, though analysts note the US was not among the countries publicly confirmed to have received advance notice. The event drew criticism from Australia, New Zealand, and Japan, and drew urgent attention from US and allied Pacific defence planners.
