US-Iran 60-Day Peace Roadmap Agreed in Switzerland
The first round of high-level US-Iran talks at the Burgenstock resort in Switzerland concluded in the early hours of June 22 with both sides agreeing to a roadmap to reach a final deal within 60 days and establishing a communication line to avoid incidents in the Strait of Hormuz, according to a joint statement by mediators Qatar and Pakistan. A committee structure covering nuclear monitoring and sanctions was also agreed. The talks followed the June 17 signing of the Islamabad Memorandum of Understanding by US President Trump and Iranian President Pezeshkian. However, the framework is under immediate strain: Iran re-declared the strait closed on June 20 citing continued Israeli strikes in Lebanon as a violation of the MOU, and as of June 22 commercial shipping transits remain sharply reduced. Iranian Foreign Minister Araghchi stated that major progress had been made on Lebanon; VP Vance said negotiators had made substantial progress on keeping the strait open and on a demining coordination mechanism.
◈ A structured 60-day timeline toward a final US-Iran agreement could accelerate Strait of Hormuz reopening and reshape regional energy security calculus for defence logistics and satellite launch insurance pricing across the Gulf.
