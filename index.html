Starship Flight 13 Aborts at Ignition
SpaceX suffered an abort of Starship Flight 13 on Jul 16 when several of Booster 20s 33 Raptor 3 engines failed to ignite at T-0, triggering an automatic launch abort -- the first full-stack ignition abort in the Starship program. Two malfunctioning Raptor engines are being removed and replaced. SpaceX has retargeted the next launch attempt for no earlier than Jul 23, 2026 at 10:45 PM UTC from Orbital Launch Pad 2 at Starbase, Texas (Space.com, RocketLaunch.Live, Jul 19-21). The mission remains intended to deploy 20 Starlink V3 satellites on a suborbital test flight, marking a key milestone for the next-generation constellation.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A repeated Starship abort delays Starlink v3 deployment timelines and gives Amazon LEO a marginal window to close the broadband LEO capability gap, adding uncertainty to SpaceX revenue projections tied to next-generation satellite throughput.
