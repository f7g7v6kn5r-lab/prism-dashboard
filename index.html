US-Iran ceasefire collapses amid fresh strikes
The United States launched new airstrikes against Iran for a second consecutive night as of Jul 9, 2026, after President Trump declared the Islamabad Memorandum of Understanding over, following Iranian attacks on commercial shipping in the Strait of Hormuz, per US CENTCOM and multiple wire sources. Iran's IRGC retaliated with missile and drone strikes targeting US military bases in Kuwait and Bahrain; Iran's army also separately claimed drone strikes on a site in Qatar, though Qatar confirmed only a high-security alert and did not confirm any airspace breach. Iran additionally fired 10 ballistic missiles at Jordan's Al Azraq Air Base, of which Jordanian air defences intercepted eight, with missile debris falling but no casualties reported. Air raid sirens were activated in Kuwait, Bahrain, Qatar, and Jordan. CNN reported as of Jul 9 that the US was striking and then pausing to avoid escalation while negotiators continued to meet behind the scenes, and that Tehran contacted US officials seeking a new deal after the latest US strikes began.
