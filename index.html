Starship Flight 13 Aborts at Ignition
SpaceX Starship Flight 13 suffered an automated abort at T-0 on July 16, 2026 after multiple Raptor 3 engines on Booster 20 failed to ignite, as confirmed by SpaceX and reported by Space.com. Elon Musk stated two Raptor engines would be removed and replaced, and the next attempt is now scheduled no earlier than July 20, 2026 at 10:45 PM UTC, per RocketLaunch.Live and Space Launch Schedule (as of July 17, 2026). The abort scrubs but does not ground the vehicle; SpaceX is actively preparing for the rescheduled attempt. The mission will carry 20 functioning Starlink V3 satellites on a suborbital profile, not a dedicated Starlink deployment mission, and represents the second flight of the Starship V3 configuration.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A grounded Starship delays SpaceX's v3 Starlink deployment cadence and keeps pressure on competing LEO broadband operators and launch providers at a time when Gulf conflict is amplifying demand for resilient satellite communications.
