The US-Iran conflict has crossed into a structurally new phase, with US CENTCOM completing a third round of strikes on Iran targeting approximately 140 Iranian military targets (per CENTCOM, July 11 2026), the IRGC launching retaliatory missiles and drones against five Gulf states -- UAE, Bahrain, Qatar, Kuwait and Oman -- and Iran declaring the Strait of Hormuz closed until further notice after firing on Cyprus-flagged container ship M/V GFS Galaxy. The strait is effectively closed to commercial shipping as of July 11 2026, with only approximately 34 transits recorded against a pre-crisis baseline of 88 per day (PortWatch, July 5 2026). The tension sits between two contradictory signals: defence primes LMT, RTX, NOC and LHX all closed modestly higher at their last session, suggesting markets priced in escalation risk as a revenue catalyst, yet the simultaneous IRGC strikes on Qatar and the UAE -- where critical US logistics and forward-basing assets are concentrated -- introduce a physical threat to the very infrastructure that sustains US power projection in CENTCOM, meaning the upside thesis for primes could be undercut if basing access degrades. The single most consequential indicator in the next 24 to 72 hours is whether Iran maintains the Hormuz closure or reopens the strait under diplomatic pressure -- Oman is mediating and has drafted a two-corridor proposal (CNN, July 11 2026) -- as a sustained closure would drive energy price shocks that compress defence budgets across European and Gulf allies simultaneously, while a reopening paired with resumed nuclear talks would mark the ceiling of this escalation cycle.

BREAKING Middle East CRITICAL · 95
Iran shuts Hormuz, strikes five Gulf states
Iran declared the Strait of Hormuz closed until further notice on July 11 2026 after the IRGC fired on and heavily damaged Cyprus-flagged container ship M/V GFS Galaxy, forcing its crew to abandon ship (CENTCOM, July 11 2026). US CENTCOM then launched a third round of strikes against approximately 140 Iranian military targets. The IRGC subsequently launched retaliatory missile and drone attacks against five Gulf states: UAE, Bahrain, Qatar, Kuwait and Oman (Al Jazeera, Euronews, July 12 2026). The brief's list of five states is confirmed but the named set should be corrected: the states targeted were UAE, Bahrain, Qatar, Kuwait and Oman -- not UAE, Bahrain and Qatar alone as originally implied. The IRGC specifically claimed strikes on Al-Udeid Air Base in Qatar, Prince Hassan Air Base in Jordan, a US radar site in Kuwait, and drone sites in Oman's Musandam governorate. Qatar's military intercepted incoming ballistic missiles; UAE air defences engaged but later said missile threats were outside its borders (UAE MoD, July 12 2026).
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A sustained Hormuz closure constitutes the most severe maritime chokepoint event in decades, directly threatening US forward-basing logistics, Gulf ally infrastructure, and global energy supply — with immediate demand implications for naval ISR, missile defence replenishment and expeditionary force-protection systems across all major defence primes.
