Starship Flight 13 Targets Raptor Relight Gate
SpaceX has targeted Starship Flight 13 for launch from Starbase, Texas no earlier than 6:45 p.m. EDT (2245 GMT) on July 16, 2026, with a 90-minute window, per Space.com (July 15). The mission -- the second flight of the Starship V3 configuration -- carries 20 functioning Starlink V3 satellites as a suborbital payload, not mass simulators; the satellites will briefly deploy, extend solar arrays, and attempt ground-station contact before reentering on the same suborbital trajectory as Starship, per SpaceNews (July 13). The primary upper-stage engineering objective is a single Raptor engine relight while in space, per NextSpaceFlight. The FAA closed its Flight 12 mishap investigation and accepted SpaceX findings on July 13, clearing launch preparations to proceed.
