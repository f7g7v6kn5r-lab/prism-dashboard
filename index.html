BREAKING North America CRITICAL · 95
Starship Flight 13 Aborts on Engine Anomaly
SpaceX aborted Starship Flight 13 at T-0 on Jul 16 when four of Booster 20s 33 Raptor 3 engines failed to ignite, triggering an automated abort. The vehicle did not lift off. Elon Musk confirmed on Jul 17 that two Raptor engines will be removed and replaced, and that the most probable next launch window is early next week, targeting around Jul 20. The abort sent SpaceX stock down more than 3 percent in after-hours trading on Jul 16; no sources confirm the share price fell below the IPO price of 135 dollars or that 1 trillion dollars in market value is at risk -- those specific claims are unconfirmed and should be treated with caution.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A failure to reattempt and successfully fly Starship within a credible near-term window would delay the Starlink v3 deployment schedule and compress the revenue trajectory that underpins the valuation case for the largest IPO in financial history.

US Bombs Iran at Highest Rate, Eyes Expansion
The US military campaign has entered its most intensive phase since hostilities resumed, with seven consecutive nights of strikes completed as of Jul 17 (CENTCOM). The June 17 memorandum of understanding has collapsed: President Trump declared it over on Jul 8 at the NATO summit in Ankara, and both sides have accused the other of violations. The MOU is not described by any credible source as having been formally declared void by both sides simultaneously -- rather, the US declared it over and Iran said it would not comply if the US breached its obligations. Iran is attacking commercial vessels and UAE-flagged tankers in the Strait of Hormuz, not the Bab el-Mandeb; the 60-day negotiating window referenced in the MOU has not elapsed -- the MOU collapsed approximately three weeks into that period.

An expanding US air campaign targeting Iranian energy infrastructure raises the probability of prolonged Strait of Hormuz closure, directly threatening global energy logistics and satellite-tracked maritime insurance markets that defence and space operators depend on.
