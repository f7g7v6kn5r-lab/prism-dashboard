BREAKING Middle East CRITICAL · 95
Iran Strikes LNG Tanker in Strait of Hormuz
Two commercial vessels were struck by projectiles in the Strait of Hormuz on Jul 7, per UK Maritime Trade Operations. One vessel, the Qatari LNG tanker Al Rekayyat, caught fire off the coast of Oman; a second vessel, a Saudi tanker named Wedyan, was also targeted, with Saudi Arabia and Qatar both formally blaming Iran. Iranian state television implied Tehran carried out the assault but issued no official claim. The US responded by revoking a general licence authorising Iranian oil sales, calling the attacks wholly unacceptable. Iran-US indirect talks on a final deal were reported to be on hold pending the conclusion of funeral ceremonies for Supreme Leader Khamenei, with the next negotiating session not yet scheduled as of Jul 7.
