US-Iran exchange strikes over Strait of Hormuz
The US military conducted strikes on Friday June 27 against Iranian missile and drone storage locations and coastal radar sites after Iran launched at least four one-way attack drones at ships transiting the Strait of Hormuz on Thursday, one of which struck the Singapore-flagged cargo vessel M/V Ever Lovely. Iran's IRGC subsequently launched retaliatory strikes on US positions in the region and struck a tanker in the Strait on Saturday. Both sides accuse the other of ceasefire violations. The exchange is the first military action by either party since Trump and Iranian President Pezeshkian signed the Islamabad Memorandum of Understanding on June 17, which established a 60-day ceasefire and committed Iran to allowing free passage of commercial vessels through the Strait. The dispute centres on competing interpretations of MOU clause 5, under which Iran claims sole authority over navigation arrangements in the Strait.
