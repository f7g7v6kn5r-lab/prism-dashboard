Israeli troops uncovered an underground Hezbollah drone facility and launch site buried beneath the village of Majdal Zoun in southern Lebanon, approximately six kilometres from the Israeli border, during an operation by reservist commandos and paratroopers earlier in June 2026. US and Iran agree 60-day deal roadmap at Swiss talks
The United States and Iran concluded the first round of high-level talks at the Burgenstock resort near Lake Lucerne, Switzerland, on Jun 21-22, 2026, agreeing on a roadmap toward a final deal within 60 days under the framework of the Islamabad Memorandum of Understanding, which was signed on Jun 17 -- not at Burgenstock. The MOU was signed by President Trump at a candlelit dinner outside Paris and by Iranian President Pezeshkian remotely. The talks were led by US Vice President JD Vance and Iranian parliament Speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf. Mediators Pakistan and Qatar confirmed the first round concluded in a positive and constructive atmosphere, producing a High Level Committee for political oversight and three technical working groups covering nuclear issues, sanctions, and monitoring and dispute resolution. A de-confliction cell was also established to prevent incidents in the Strait of Hormuz and to support adherence to the termination of military operations in Lebanon. The talks had been briefly delayed from Friday Jun 20 to Sunday Jun 21 after Israel launched strikes in Lebanon following the deaths of four of its soldiers. No source confirmed a walkout by Iranian negotiators over Trump remarks regarding Lebanon; a senior US diplomat explicitly rejected reports that Iran had left negotiations (CNBC, Al Jazeera, Jun 22, 2026).
◈ A verified Hormuz de-confliction mechanism directly reduces near-term shipping disruption risk and could stabilise energy prices affecting supply chains for space launch and defence procurement across the Gulf region.
