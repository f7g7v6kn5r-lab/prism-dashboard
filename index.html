Starship Flight 13 Aborts at Ignition
SpaceX aborted Starship Flight 13 during the ignition sequence on July 17 when an automated hold was triggered at T-0 as Raptor engines began to light on Booster 20; SpaceX subsequently announced it would replace two Raptor engines before the next attempt. As of July 19, the next launch attempt is scheduled no earlier than July 20, 2026 at 10:45 PM UTC from Orbital Launch Pad 2 at Starbase, Texas. The mission profile -- deploying 20 Starlink V3 satellites on a suborbital trajectory -- is unchanged. The abort does not affect the planned flight objectives and the vehicle retains GO status per tracking sources updated July 19.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A repeated Starship ignition anomaly directly threatens the deployment schedule for Starlink v3 and raises program risk questions that SpaceX investors and Amazon LEO competitors will monitor closely for schedule relief.
