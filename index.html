BREAKING Middle East CRITICAL · 97
Iran Fires Missiles at Ships in Strait of Hormuz
Irans Revolutionary Guards fired missiles at commercial ships transiting the Strait of Hormuz on Tuesday July 7, striking the Qatari LNG tanker Al Rekayyat off the Omani coast and causing a fire; a second vessel believed to be a Saudi-flagged crude tanker was also damaged. Iran did not officially claim the attacks, though Iranian state television implied responsibility, citing the vessels ignoring warnings. Qatar condemned the strike as an unacceptable violation of international law and held Iran fully legally responsible. The attacks came as active US-Iran peace negotiations continue -- the Strait of Hormuz is a key sticking point in those talks -- and on the day Trump warned Iran to make a deal or face the US finishing the job. No casualties were reported as of the latest available reporting (July 7).
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
Resumed Iranian interdiction of commercial shipping at the Strait of Hormuz directly threatens the corridor through which 38 percent of global seaborne crude oil passed before the prior Hormuz crisis, creating acute risk to energy markets and renewed demand for maritime patrol, mine countermeasure, and space-based maritime surveillance capabilities.

BREAKING Asia-Pacific CRITICAL · 95
China Tests Nuclear SLBM in South Pacific
China test-fired a submarine-launched ballistic missile from the South China Sea on July 6, with Taiwan identifying it as a JL-2 and other analysts assessing it may be a JL-2 or the newer JL-3; the PLAN did not publicly identify the missile type or launch submarine. The missile flew over the northern Philippines and landed in the Pacific between Nauru and Tonga. The test drew condemnation from the US, Australia, New Zealand and Japan, and support from Russia, which was simultaneously beginning its annual Joint Sea naval exercise with China in Qingdao. New Zealand raised the nuclear-weapons-free zone concern under the Treaty of Rarotonga; however, independent analysis as of July 7 indicates China signed Protocols II and III of that treaty in 1987 and the test did not constitute a violation of those protocols.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A Chinese nuclear SLBM demonstration in the South Pacific directly raises the salience of undersea domain awareness, satellite-based missile launch detection, and allied ballistic missile defence investment across Japan, Australia, and South Korea.

BREAKING Europe CRITICAL · 93
Russia Kills 28 in Kyiv Strikes, Ukraine Air Defences Strained
Russia launched a mass missile and drone assault on Kyiv on the night of July 5-6, killing at least 22 people according to multiple wire reports as of July 6-7, with earlier tallies from a separate July 1-2 strike reaching 27-30. The July 6 assault involved 68 missiles and 351 drones; all 29 ballistic missiles struck their targets, exposing Ukraines critical shortage of Patriot interceptors ahead of the NATO summit in Ankara. Ukraine responded by launching drones toward Russia, with TASS reporting Russia shot down 519 Ukrainian drones overnight into Monday. President Zelenskyy renewed his appeal for additional air-defence interceptors at the summit, stating the insufficient supply of interceptor missiles was the reason Ukraine could not stop the ballistic missiles.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
