US Strikes 90 Iranian Targets; Iran Hits Gulf Bases
The United States conducted a second consecutive day of precision strikes against approximately 90 Iranian military targets, with CENTCOM releasing operational video footage and confirming the strikes were aimed at further degrading Irans ability to threaten freedom of navigation in the Strait of Hormuz (as of Jul 9 2026, Bloomberg, RFE/RL). Iran retaliated with drone and missile strikes against US bases in Kuwait and Bahrain, and also launched attack drones targeting sites in Qatar, according to multiple corroborating sources. The tit-for-tat exchange has severely strained ceasefire efforts; Trump declared the ceasefire over at the NATO summit in Ankara, calling Iranian leaders scum and sick people, though he stopped short of a formal declaration that the MOU itself is legally terminated, and his negotiators Witkoff and Kushner were not ruled out from continuing talks (Axios, NPR, Jul 8 2026). Iran also struck a US-linked base in Jordan, which Jordanian forces said they intercepted (RFE/RL, Jul 9 2026).
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A two-day exchange of direct strikes between the US and Iran targeting Gulf state hosts signals the conflict has moved beyond a bilateral confrontation into a regional war footing, creating immediate force protection and basing risk for every defence operator with assets in Bahrain, Kuwait, and Qatar.
