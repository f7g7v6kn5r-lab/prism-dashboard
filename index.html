China Recovers Orbital Booster With Long March 10B
China launched its Long March 10B rocket on Jul 10 2026 and successfully recovered the first stage booster using a sea-based net-capture system aboard the recovery vessel Linghangzhe, making China the second country in history to recover an orbital-class rocket booster after launch (SpaceNews, Jul 10 2026). The milestone, achieved by state-owned CASC, is distinct from SpaceX and Blue Origin recovery methods in that it uses a net-arrestment approach with no landing legs. CASC stated it intends to refly the recovered booster before end of 2026. The brief context is accurate but should note that Scientific American and multiple sources confirm Blue Origin -- not only SpaceX -- also holds orbital-class booster reuse capability, placing China alongside two US organisations rather than solely SpaceX.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A reusable Chinese orbital booster compresses the cost gap between Western and Chinese launch services, threatening commercial and government launch market share for RKLB, SpaceX, and eventually Blue Origin over a multi-year horizon.
