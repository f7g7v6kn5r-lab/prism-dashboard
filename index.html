BREAKING Middle East CRITICAL · 95
US Strikes Iran After Hormuz Tanker Attacks
CENTCOM struck more than 80 Iranian military targets overnight Jul 7-8 -- including air defence systems, command and control networks, coastal radar sites, anti-ship missile capabilities, and more than 60 IRGC small boats -- after Iran attacked three commercial vessels in the Strait of Hormuz (a Qatari LNG tanker and a Saudi-flagged supertanker on Jul 7, and a third vessel shortly after). The US Treasury simultaneously revoked a general licence for Iranian oil sales that had been issued under the June 17 Islamabad Memorandum of Understanding. Iran retaliated by launching missiles and drones at US military facilities at Bahrain Salman Port and Ali Al Salem air base in Kuwait; Bahrain and Kuwait both activated air defence sirens. No confirmed civilian deaths have been reported from the US strikes. As of the morning of Jul 8, Trump declared the ceasefire over at the NATO Ankara summit, though he stated US negotiators may continue talks.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
Active US kinetic engagement against Iranian military infrastructure in a critical global energy chokepoint elevates systemic risk across oil markets, Gulf defence procurement, and space-based maritime surveillance demand in a manner that could trigger emergency contracting for ISR and strike-enabling systems.

BREAKING Asia-Pacific CRITICAL · 90
China Pacific Ballistic Missile Test Alarms Region
On Jul 6, the Chinese Peoples Liberation Army Navy conducted its first publicly acknowledged submarine-launched ballistic missile test into the Pacific, firing a JL-2 or JL-3 missile carrying a dummy warhead from a nuclear-powered submarine in the South China Sea to designated waters in the South Pacific Nuclear Free Zone -- a flight path of roughly 7,300 km. The test drew criticism from the US, Australia, Japan, and New Zealand; Australia and Japan received advance notification but the US received only hours of notice. Analysts at CSIS described it as the first time Beijing has publicly demonstrated a strategic nuclear strike capability launched from a submarine into the open Pacific. No DF-17 hypersonic glide vehicle footage was reported in connection with this event; the test involved a submarine-launched ballistic missile only.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
China's dual signal — a live Pacific missile test plus a state media DF-17 showcase — will accelerate AUKUS, Quad, and bilateral alliance spending on missile defence, space-based early warning, and hypersonic countermeasures, directly expanding the addressable market for firms operating in those segments.
