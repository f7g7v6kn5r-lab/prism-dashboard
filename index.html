BREAKING North America CRITICAL · 87
Starship Flight 13 aborts at ignition in Texas
SpaceX attempted the 13th test flight of Starship and Super Heavy from Starbase, Texas, on July 16 with a launch window opening at 6:45 p.m. Eastern. An automatic shutdown aborted the attempt at the last second, per Space.com reporting updated at 7:10 p.m. ET on July 16. As of Jul 17, launch tracking sites list the next attempt as no earlier than July 31, 2026, though SpaceX has not confirmed that date publicly. The abort occurred before liftoff; no damage to the vehicle was reported and SpaceX is assessing the anomaly before scheduling a new window.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A repeated ignition abort on the vehicle intended to carry the first Starlink v3 satellites and eventually support Artemis lunar missions raises programmatic schedule risk for both SpaceX commercial revenue and NASA dependencies, and will draw renewed scrutiny from the FAA which only recently lifted a grounding order.

US strikes Iran for sixth consecutive night
US Central Command completed its sixth consecutive night of strikes against Iran on the evening of Jul 16, CENTCOM confirmed via social media (CNN, updated Jul 17). Iran's Revolutionary Guards claimed strikes on US radar sites in Oman. The Jun 17 Islamabad MOU between the US and Iran collapsed on Jul 8 when Trump declared it over; fighting resumed and has intensified since.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
Six nights of continuous strikes with no ceasefire signal and active Congressional war funding confirm this is no longer a containment operation — energy infrastructure targeting would mark a threshold escalation with immediate consequences for global oil supply, Gulf basing, and allied force posture.

BREAKING Strait of Hormuz / Persian Gulf CRITICAL · 90
Iran-US Diplomacy Alive as Hormuz War Rages
The Jun 17 Islamabad Memorandum of Understanding -- signed by Trump and Iranian President Pezeshkian -- collapsed on Jul 8 when Trump declared it over following mutual violations. Active fighting has resumed at scale since then. Back-channel diplomatic activity persists: Pakistan and Qatar are working to bring the sides back to the table (CNN, Jul 9), and Trump has stated Washington will continue talks if Iran requests them (Al Jazeera, Jul 10), though Iran's foreign ministry disputed that characterisation. China also called for a ceasefire and resumption of dialogue on Jul 16 (Times of Israel). The situation is one of resumed active conflict with contested and fragile diplomatic signalling, not a balance between ongoing military pressure and a functioning negotiating process.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
The coexistence of active combat and diplomatic signalling means defence operators in the Gulf must plan for rapid scenario shifts, as a sudden ceasefire or a dramatic escalation could both materially affect procurement timelines and maritime security contracts.
