BREAKING North America CRITICAL · 90
Starship Flight 13 Aborts at Ignition
SpaceX scrubbed what would have been Starship Flight 13 -- and its first Starship launch since the company went public -- after an automated abort triggered at T-0 when several Raptor 3 engines on Booster 20 failed to ignite at South Padre Island on July 16. The mission is intended to deploy 20 functioning Starlink V3 satellites on a suborbital flight profile; the satellites will not reach orbit and are expected to reenter after a brief deployment test. Elon Musk posted on July 17 that two Raptor engines will be removed and replaced, and identified early next week as the most probable timing for the next attempt. Multiple launch-tracking sources as of July 17 show a retargeted date of July 20, 2026, though that date remains subject to change.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A second consecutive high-profile Starship operational delay raises programme risk for defence and megaconstellation customers counting on heavy-lift capacity and signals that the post-IPO period introduces execution pressure alongside capital market scrutiny.
