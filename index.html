BREAKING Middle East CRITICAL · 100
US declares maritime blockade on Iran from Tuesday
The US military has announced it will begin enforcing a reinstated maritime blockade on Iran starting Tuesday 14 July at 1600 ET (2000 GMT), per a JMIC advisory reported by Reuters on 13 July 2026. The blockade covers all Iranian ports, oil terminals, and coastal areas and applies to all vessel traffic regardless of flag; neutral transit through the Strait of Hormuz to or from non-Iranian destinations will not be impeded. This follows US Navy sea drones striking Iranian targets in what CENTCOM describes as a combat first for the US Navy, and Iranian claims of retaliatory strikes on US bases in Jordan, Bahrain, Kuwait, Oman, and Qatar. The escalation represents a breakdown of the memorandum of understanding signed by Trump and Iranian president Pezeshkian on 17 June 2026, which had lifted a prior blockade on 18 June.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A formal US maritime blockade enforced against Iran is the most consequential escalation of the Gulf confrontation to date, directly threatening global energy transit through the Strait of Hormuz and generating immediate demand signals for naval ISR, mine countermeasures, counter-drone systems, and air and missile defence assets protecting forward US bases.
