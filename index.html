BREAKING North America CRITICAL · 90
Northrop MRV-1 robot launches on decade mission
SpaceX launched Northrop Grumman SpaceLogistics Mission Robotic Vehicle 1 (MRV-1) and three Mission Extension Pods (MEPs) on Tuesday July 22 2026 from Space Launch Complex 40 at Cape Canaveral Space Force Station on a Falcon 9 at 5:15 p.m. ET (as of July 21 2026, Satellite Today). All payloads were confirmed deployed. The MRV carries two robotic arms developed with the Naval Research Lab and NASA and is designed to attach up to 30 MEPs to GEO satellites over its operational life; each MEP can extend a 2,000 kg satellite lifespan by up to six years. The three MEPs on this flight are contracted to service satellites for Intelsat and Optus. The booster, B1069, was expended and not recovered.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
Successful deployment validates the on-orbit servicing market as a multi-decade revenue stream for Northrop Grumman and signals to defence operators that ageing GEO assets no longer face binary end-of-life decisions.
