BREAKING Middle East CRITICAL · 95
UN halts Hormuz escorts after cargo ship struck
The UN IMO temporarily paused -- not suspended -- its voluntary vessel evacuation operation through the Strait of Hormuz on 25 June 2026 after Singapore-flagged cargo ship Ever Lovely was struck on the starboard side by an Iranian drone near the Omani coast, confirmed by two US officials to Reuters. The struck vessel was not part of the IMO evacuation framework and was navigating the Omani-coast route rather than the IRGC-designated northern route; crew, vessel and cargo were reported safe and the ship departed the strait. IMO Secretary-General Arsenio Dominguez said the pause is temporary pending confirmation that safety guarantees are in place for ships on the evacuation list. The IMO evacuation initiative, launched on 24 June 2026, had been a voluntary option covering two routes -- one via Iranian waters and one via Omani waters -- with US oversight. The Islamabad MOU itself remains in force; technical follow-on talks are scheduled to begin 30 June 2026.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
Suspension of UN Hormuz escorts re-prices maritime risk insurance premiums and threatens Gulf energy export continuity, a scenario that directly elevates demand for naval drone surveillance assets and rapid-reaction maritime defence systems.
