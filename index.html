Pentagon seeks cheaper drones to replace MQ-9 Reaper
The Defense Innovation Unit issued a solicitation on July 8, 2026 for a Massed Modular Aircraft (MMA) -- described as a drone to execute missions the MQ-9A performs today -- following the loss of nearly 30 Reapers in combat against Iran per a May 13 Congressional Research Service report. The MMA is not described in the notice as a direct Reaper replacement but is envisioned for strike, ISR and electronic warfare missions; the solicitation calls for full-scale prototype flight testing within 21 months of award and an IOC of 20 mission-ready aircraft by FY2031 (Breaking Defense, Jul 8). A pending tasking order is not required to confirm the structural shift: the DIU solicitation itself constitutes a formal procurement action.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
This procurement pivot represents a significant and immediate market-creation event for smaller drone manufacturers and defence technology integrators, with DIU notices historically accelerating from requirement to contract award faster than traditional Pentagon acquisition pathways.
