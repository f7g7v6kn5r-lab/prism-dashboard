BREAKING Middle East CRITICAL · 95
Iran Strikes Hormuz Tankers; US Reimposes Oil Sanctions
Iran struck three commercial vessels in the Strait of Hormuz on 7 July 2026: a Qatari LNG tanker (Al Rekayyat) and a Saudi supertanker (Wedyan) were hit first, with a third vessel struck by drone hours later (UKMTO, Reuters, 7 Jul 2026). In response, the US revoked a licence authorising Iranian oil sales that had been granted under the interim deal -- a targeted sanction rollback rather than a full reimposition of the pre-deal sanctions regime -- and CENTCOM subsequently announced more than 80 strikes on Iranian military sites near the strait. The attacks occurred while US-Iran peace talks were continuing and while the interim deal remained nominally in place as of 8 July 2026; the deal has not been declared collapsed by either side.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
Renewed Hormuz interdiction combined with reimposed oil sanctions creates a high-volatility environment for Gulf-based defence logistics, energy shipping insurance, and any operator with assets or contracts dependent on Strait of Hormuz transit.

China conducted a submarine-launched ballistic missile test on 6 July 2026 -- not 7-8 July as the brief stated -- firing a dummy warhead from a nuclear-powered submarine from the South China Sea into the South Pacific, marking China's first publicly acknowledged SLBM test to travel that far into the Pacific.
