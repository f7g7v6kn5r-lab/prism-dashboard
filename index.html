Space Force Triples Launch Ceiling to $17B
The US Space Force raised the maximum value of its National Security Space Launch Phase 3 Lane 1 contract from 5.6 billion to 17 billion dollars, an increase of 11.4 billion, expanding awards across SpaceX, Blue Origin, and five other providers. The decision reflects surging demand for assured national security launch capacity at a moment when contested space operations and rapid satellite constellation deployment have become strategic priorities.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS

BREAKING North America CRITICAL · 88
Starship Flight 13 Aborts at Ignition
SpaceX Starship Flight 13 suffered an automated abort at ignition at Starbase, Texas on July 16, 2026, when several Raptor 3 engines on Booster 20 failed to light, triggering the onboard flight computer to call a hold at T-0. SpaceX CEO Elon Musk confirmed two Raptor engines are being removed and replaced; as of July 19, SpaceX has scheduled a second launch attempt for July 20, 2026 at 10:45 PM UTC. The abort represents a delay -- not a mission failure -- to both Starlink v3 satellite deployment and the Pentagons growing dependency on Starship-class vehicles for large-payload national security missions. Flight 13 is the second flight of Starship V3 and will carry 20 functioning Starlink V3 satellites on a suborbital profile.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
An extended Starship stand-down would delay Starlink v3 deployment timelines and expose the concentration risk in US launch strategy at precisely the moment the Space Force is expanding contract ceilings to diversify that dependency.
