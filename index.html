BREAKING North America CRITICAL · 90
Starship Flight 13 aborts at ignition
SpaceX Starship Flight 13 suffered a post-ignition abort on Jul 16 when four Raptor 3 engines on Booster 20 failed to ignite as planned, triggering an automatic abort (Spaceflight Now, Jul 16). The abort did not involve Booster 20 as a distinct anomaly category: on-screen telemetry showed engine non-starts, not a booster-level anomaly. Elon Musk confirmed two Raptors will be removed and replaced, with the next launch attempt retargeted for no earlier than Jul 20, 2026 (Space.com / SpaceX via X, Jul 17). The payload is 20 Starlink V3 satellites on a suborbital trajectory; they are not being deployed into operational orbit and are expected to burn up on reentry. SpaceX stock fell more than 3 percent in after-hours trading on Jul 16 following the abort.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A repeated Starship abort cycle delays v3 Starlink capacity expansion and prolongs the window in which rival LEO operators including Amazon LEO and ASTS can capture enterprise and government connectivity contracts ahead of SpaceX scaling.
