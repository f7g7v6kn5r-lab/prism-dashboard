BREAKING Middle East CRITICAL · 90
UN halts Hormuz escort after cargo ship attack
The IMO temporarily paused its voluntary ship evacuation operation through the Strait of Hormuz on June 25 after the Singapore-flagged Evergreen container ship Ever Lovely was struck by a projectile near Oman, according to Reuters and Seatrade Maritime (June 25-26). Two US officials told Reuters that Iran fired on the vessel; Iran has not claimed responsibility. IMO Secretary-General Dominguez confirmed the struck ship was not operating under the IMO evacuation framework and said the pause was to reconfirm safety guarantees for ships on the evacuation list. The attack followed an IRGC warning issued the same day that vessels transiting without Iranian permission were doing so illegally. As of June 26, Trump had publicly attributed the strike to Iran -- per a Wikipedia summary citing a June 26 Trump statement -- and Secretary of State Rubio, wrapping a Gulf tour, said unauthorized tolls or blockades would be unacceptable. The US-Iran memorandum of understanding, signed June 17-18, established a 60-day toll-free reopening of the Strait; the MOU itself remains in force but the IMO evacuation operation is paused pending clarification of safety guarantees.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
An IMO escort suspension at Hormuz is a direct threat to global energy and shipping flows and signals that the ceasefire risk premium in maritime insurance, ISR satellite tasking, and naval patrol asset demand will remain elevated rather than compressing on peace deal optimism.
