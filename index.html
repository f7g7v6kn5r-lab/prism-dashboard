BREAKING Strait of Hormuz / Middle East CRITICAL · 95
Strait of Hormuz Reopens Under US-Iran Deal
The United States and Iran signed a 14-point memorandum of understanding on June 15-17, 2026, under which the US lifted its naval blockade of Iranian ports and the Strait of Hormuz was authorized to reopen, with a 60-day window for nuclear negotiations (NBC News, June 17, 2026; NPR, June 15, 2026). Pakistani mediation was confirmed. However, the main central route of the Strait remains closed pending mine clearance; ships have been transiting the narrower northern and southern routes (CBS News, June 18, 2026). The MOU was signed remotely; Pakistan's PM Sharif cancelled a planned in-person Switzerland signing visit because the document had already entered into force electronically (AFP via CBS News, June 19, 2026).
◈ Reopening of the Strait of Hormuz removes the most acute chokepoint risk to global energy supply chains, directly easing pressure on defence logistics budgets and stabilising fuel cost assumptions for space and defence operators across the Gulf.
