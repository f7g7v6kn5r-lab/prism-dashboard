On July 6, 2026, the Chinese PLA Navy conducted its first publicly acknowledged strategic submarine-launched ballistic missile test into the Pacific Ocean, launching from a nuclear-powered submarine in the South China Sea toward designated international waters in the South Pacific, per Army Recognition and CSIS. The test validated Chinas sea-based second-strike nuclear capability. Chinese state media characterised it as routine annual training targeting no specific country. Australia, New Zealand, Japan, and the US condemned the test.
