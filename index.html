BREAKING North America CRITICAL · 92
Starship Flight 13 Aborts at Engine Ignition
SpaceX attempted the 13th test flight of Starship and Super Heavy from Starbase, Texas on Jul 16, but an automated abort triggered at T-0 when four of the Super Heavy boosters 33 Raptor 3 engines failed to ignite. Elon Musk confirmed on Jul 17 that two engines will be removed and replaced, and said the most probable next launch attempt is early next week; no specific date has been set. The abort occurred at the very end of the countdown, not post-ignition with engines failing mid-sequence. The mission was also intended to deploy 20 Starlink v3 satellites on a suborbital trajectory, not to place them in orbit.
◆ PRISM ANALYSIS
A delayed Flight 13 directly pushes back the operational Starlink v3 constellation timeline and prolongs uncertainty over Starships readiness for Artemis, point-to-point cargo, and military heavy-lift missions that defence planners are already pricing into long-range programmes.
